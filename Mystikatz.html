Mystikats was made by Playhut amidst the Bratz controversy and aimed to capitalise on the hype for fashion forward, fierce teenagers. They were touted as 'half girl, half kat'

with a range of gimmicks only seen in the later hald of the 2010's. There is a recorded difference in the initial release of dolls, with one version for international release and the release version that was exclusive to Australia.

The company, for reasons unknown to the public, decided that the future Mystikats doll releases would only be released in Australia and this marks where the history is a bit lost.

Another Australian toy enthusiast, Tess Fabled, has made her own article in regards to her research into the Mystikats brand.
