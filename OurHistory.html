It all began with our queer & disabled founder, Ty Lavine, who immidiately fell in love with the world wide phoenomenom Monster High when it was announced. She became an instant fan and that love has remained ever since.

Having watched G4 My Little Pony in high school, and after the end of G2 Monster High and the brand went into hiatus, this lead to our founder going back to previous childhood connections, which included the third generation of My Little Pony.

This lead to her researching that generation of My Little Pony and discovering the whole MLP (My Little Pony) community, knew that any offiical MLP toys could be sought after and sold.

Her first op shop MLP find, an okay condition Royalette was found and bought. After this, it lead her to the realization that outside of Europe's, Turtle Island's and the UK's tracked releases, Australia

There was no knowledge for what was sold in Australia beyond community veterans and their memories, what they had managed to keep and any personal records (email trades, forum trades, etc) for the first and second generation were a mystery.

There was more concrete information for generation 3 and after, leading to Ty wondering what Australia's MLP history was and what had been forgotten, or fragmented and buried.

This idea for some kind of records for history in her early 20's, and the idea eventually evolved into the early stages of HTSOA at age 23.

While she wasn't working on it officially for many years, her curiosity and determinaion still lead her to researching, connecting with community members, asking questions,

and as the years passed, the clear snapshots of the Monster High dolls she'd seen in stores began to dissapear, or they began to be replaced with the memory of her remembering those dolls, not the memory itself.

and after many tumoltuous years, where she did not know if she would survive, she did. She asked members of her toy friendship group for a name for her society, many helped and with her friend's help, Candii, suggested 'The Historical Toy Society of Australia'.

It was perfect, and that has been it's name ever since.

And at age 27, her ideas evolving along with more research with a fantastic environment and the support she has always needed, began the first step to archiving and finding out this land's forgotten history. She began this very website with the help of her wonderful bother, and

has been updating ever since.
