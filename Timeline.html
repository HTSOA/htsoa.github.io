The timeline as we know it.

As a lot of knowledge from pre colonisation times has had efforts to be erased and replaced, the only sources online are from a western perspective so it might be inaccurate.

We are looking for first nations people with this knowledge to share and combat the misinformation spread by western sources.

First Nations peoples first arrived here approximately 65'000 years ago from South East Asia. Knowledge from this time is scarce, but it is said by researchers (not by first nations peoples themselves), the evidence says they normally consisted of shells, pandanus leaves, grass, emu feathers, clay, forked branches and many other natural materials.

Known categories of toys are spears, balls, weet weets, spinning tops, dolls, rattles, wooden stringrays, toy boomerangs, pandanus, toy bows and arrows and kundi kundi. From what I've been able to find

there are hadly, if any, toys from pre colonasation periods. Some games managed to be preserved as they were passed down from generation to generation, being taught and remembered.

The first colonists arrived in 1877, and drastically changed how first nations kids played and how their toys were made. A lot of the toys and knowledge from this time managed to be preserved, albeit many had been altered from their origins as First Nations people

were forced away from their country and communities, often into slavery through 'schools' which erased their identity, culture, etc or massacred.
